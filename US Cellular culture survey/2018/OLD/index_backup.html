Welcome & Objectives

The 2018 Culture Survey results are in! Take this opportunity to leverage associate insights to build on the strengths and talents of your team and explore opportunities for improvement.

The website provides resources that will help you:

  • Evaluate survey results and reflect on your team’s performance
  • Leverage any effective efforts that are yielding positive impact
  • Adjust any in-progress action plans, as needed, in order to drive associate engagement

Timeline

Officers, in partnership with directors, are expected to communicate local results by December 15. Officers will plan any additional or alternative efforts that may be required to maintain or increase engagement. As always, it is expected that you communicate the survey results with your teams; how you do that is up to you. Use your best judgment to determine how best to share these results in a fashion that makes sense to you and your organization.

2018 Approach

This year’s check-in survey allowed us to assess the progress that we’re making on the action plans we developed last year. Just as important, it provides an opportunity to collect “localized” insights specific to functional areas and leaders.

The Questionnaire Design

The survey measured associate perceptions in two ways:

  • Company-wide: We narrowed the scope of the survey this year to focus on company-wide engagement components.
  • Functional-level: The survey was localized to target specific areas of interest within each functional group selected by vice presidents and administered to their downstream hierarchies.

Engagement Score

This year, the ‘engagement score’ will not be available. The focus of the check-in is to gauge movement on in‐progress actions being taken to improve opportunities from the previous year’s assessment.

Prepare for Action

Review these recommendations as you prepare to reflect and respond to your results. Leverage your HR Business Partner as a coach during the action planning process, if needed.

Evaluate

  • Reflect on your results
  • Identify strengths and areas of opportunity
  • Design a brief summary of results and observations for your group

Collaborate & Adjust

Officers: Share a summary of results and observations with your directors

  • Recognize and leverage effective efforts and/or best practices
  • Call out any in-progress action plans that are in need of remediation

Office and Directors: Revise action plans as needed and gain agreement on upcoming action-taking priorities

Inspire Positive Change

  • Communicate your insights on functional results to downstream teams
  • Continue to inspire your team to gain momentum on associate engagement levels

Officers will determine the most appropriate method of sharing local results with team members(email, town hall meetings, teleconference, WebEx, audio, etc.)

Role & Responsibilities

You are responsible for making choices that support efficient and effective team dialogue. Customize your approach by selecting the options that make sense for your team. These may include:

Officers

Share a summary of the results and your initial reflections with your director team

If appropriate, communicate the need for increased or changed efforts to in-progress action plans

If no changes are necessary, highlight effective efforts and/or best practices

Partner with directors to update/revise plans as necessary and gain agreement on this year’s action-taking priorities

Officers and Directors

Develop a communication plan to convey your reactions to the functional results to your downstream teams

Report Eligibility

Report eligibility is determined by the criteria listed below. Officers & Directors can potentially receive any combination of reports depending on the number of responses received. Most items on the checklists included below are optional.

Data Report: Officers & Directors with 5 or more responses

  • Local survey data
  • Data is grouped by company-wide and functional items (if available) to facilitate easy understanding of the results and inform of any changes to opportunity areas identified from last year’s survey

Comment Report: Officers & Directors with more than 25 responses

  • All comments made by a respective group
  • Comments are grouped together by topics selected by associates when completing the survey

No Report: Leaders with fewer than 5 responses

  • These officers and directors rely on data and insights gathered from the reports of their upstream leaders.
